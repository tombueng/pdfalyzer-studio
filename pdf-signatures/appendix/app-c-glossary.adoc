[appendix]
= Glossary & Spec References

[.lead]
This appendix collects every acronym, term, and specification referenced throughout the book into two lookup tables.
When you encounter an unfamiliar term in a chapter, this is the place to find a concise definition.
When you need the precise specification for a standard we reference, the spec table gives you the document number, title, and where to find it.

== Glossary

[cols="2,6", options="header"]
|===
| Term | Definition

| AATL
| **Adobe Approved Trust List.** Adobe's curated list of root CA certificates trusted by Acrobat and Reader for signature validation. CAs must apply and meet Adobe's requirements to be listed. If a signer's certificate does not chain to an AATL root, Acrobat shows a warning even if the signature is cryptographically valid.

| AcroForm
| **Interactive Form Dictionary.** The PDF structure (`/AcroForm` in the document catalog) that contains all form fields, including signature fields. Defined in {iso32000-2} Section 12.7.

| AIA
| **Authority Information Access.** An X.509 v3 certificate extension ({rfc5280} Section 4.2.2.1) that provides URLs for the OCSP responder and the issuer's certificate. Used to fetch missing intermediate certificates during chain building.

| ASN.1
| **Abstract Syntax Notation One.** A standard for describing data structures used in X.509 certificates, CMS, OCSP, and TSP. Structures are encoded using DER (Distinguished Encoding Rules) for cryptographic applications.

| ByteRange
| The `/ByteRange` array in a PDF signature dictionary. Specifies which bytes of the PDF file are covered by the signature. Always four integers: `[offset1 length1 offset2 length2]`. See {iso32000-2} Section 12.8.1.

| CA
| **Certificate Authority.** An entity that issues X.509 certificates. CAs form a hierarchy: root CAs are self-signed and explicitly trusted; intermediate CAs are signed by a root or another intermediate.

| CAdES
| **CMS Advanced Electronic Signatures.** ETSI standard (EN 319 122) defining enhanced CMS signature profiles for long-term validity and legal compliance. PAdES builds on CAdES for the CMS portion of PDF signatures.

| CMS
| **Cryptographic Message Syntax.** The standard ({rfc5652}) for signing and encrypting data. PDF signatures use CMS `SignedData` as the cryptographic payload inside the `/Contents` value. Successor to PKCS#7.

| CRL
| **Certificate Revocation List.** A signed list published by a CA containing the serial numbers of revoked certificates. Defined in {rfc5280} Section 5. Checked during certificate chain validation.

| DER
| **Distinguished Encoding Rules.** A deterministic binary encoding of ASN.1 structures. All cryptographic ASN.1 (certificates, CMS, OCSP, TSP) uses DER encoding.

| DSS
| **Document Security Store.** A PDF dictionary (`/DSS` in the document catalog) defined in {iso32000-2} Section 12.8.4.3 that stores certificates, CRLs, and OCSP responses needed for offline (LTV) validation.

| ECDSA
| **Elliptic Curve Digital Signature Algorithm.** A signature algorithm based on elliptic curve cryptography. Uses smaller keys than RSA for equivalent security (e.g., 256-bit ECDSA approximates 3072-bit RSA).

| eIDAS
| **Electronic Identification, Authentication, and Trust Services.** EU Regulation (No 910/2014) that establishes a legal framework for electronic signatures, seals, and timestamps across EU member states. Defines three signature levels: simple, advanced, and qualified.

| ESS
| **Extended Security Services.** Defined in RFC 2634 and RFC 5035. The `ESS-signing-certificate-v2` attribute is required in PAdES signatures to bind the CMS signature to a specific signer certificate.

| ETSI
| **European Telecommunications Standards Institute.** The standards body that publishes PAdES (EN 319 142), CAdES (EN 319 122), and the validation standards (TS 119 102) referenced by the eIDAS regulation.

| EUTL
| **European Union Trusted List.** The official registry of Qualified Trust Service Providers (QTSPs) maintained by EU member states. Analogous to Adobe's AATL but for the EU regulatory framework.

| HSM
| **Hardware Security Module.** A physical device that stores private keys and performs cryptographic operations. Used in production signing environments to protect key material from extraction.

| Incremental Update
| A mechanism defined in {iso32000-2} Section 7.5.6 that appends new or modified objects to the end of a PDF file without rewriting the entire file. Every PDF signature is applied via an incremental update.

| LTV
| **Long-Term Validation.** The practice of embedding all data needed for signature validation (certificates, CRLs, OCSP responses) within the PDF itself, so the signature can be validated offline, indefinitely, without contacting external servers.

| MDP
| **Modification Detection and Prevention.** A PDF mechanism ({iso32000-2} Section 12.8.2.2) that specifies what changes are permitted after a certification signature is applied. Three permission levels exist (P=1, P=2, P=3).

| OCSP
| **Online Certificate Status Protocol.** A protocol ({rfc6960}) for checking certificate revocation status in real time. An OCSP responder returns `good`, `revoked`, or `unknown` for a given certificate serial number.

| OID
| **Object Identifier.** A globally unique dotted-decimal number (e.g., `1.2.840.113549.1.7.2`) that identifies algorithms, attributes, extensions, and other standardized objects in ASN.1 structures.

| PAdES
| **PDF Advanced Electronic Signatures.** ETSI standard (EN 319 142) that defines constrained profiles for PDF signatures to meet EU legal requirements. Four baseline profiles: B-B, B-T, B-LT, B-LTA.

| PKCS#7
| **Public-Key Cryptography Standards #7.** The predecessor to CMS. PDF signatures historically used the term "PKCS#7" (and the SubFilter `adbe.pkcs7.detached`), though modern implementations use CMS. The terms are often used interchangeably.

| PKCS#12
| **Public-Key Cryptography Standards #12.** A file format for storing private keys and certificates together, typically with `.p12` or `.pfx` extension. Protected by a password.

| PKIX
| **Public-Key Infrastructure (X.509).** The set of standards and protocols for managing X.509 certificates and building certificate chains. In Java, `CertPathBuilder` and `CertPathValidator` implement the PKIX algorithm.

| QTSP
| **Qualified Trust Service Provider.** Under eIDAS, a trust service provider that has been granted qualified status by an EU member state's supervisory body. Only QTSPs can issue qualified certificates and qualified timestamps.

| RSA
| **Rivest-Shamir-Adleman.** The most widely used public-key algorithm for digital signatures. Common key sizes for PDF signatures are 2048 and 4096 bits.

| SHA-256
| **Secure Hash Algorithm, 256-bit.** The recommended minimum hash algorithm for new PDF signatures. Produces a 32-byte (256-bit) digest. Part of the SHA-2 family.

| SubFilter
| The `/SubFilter` entry in a PDF signature dictionary that specifies the CMS encoding format. Values: `adbe.pkcs7.detached` (classic), `ETSI.CAdES.detached` (PAdES), `adbe.pkcs7.sha1` (deprecated), `adbe.x509.rsa_sha1` (deprecated).

| TSA
| **Timestamp Authority.** A trusted server that issues RFC 3161 timestamp tokens. The timestamp token proves that specific data existed at a specific point in time.

| TSP
| **Time-Stamp Protocol.** The protocol defined in {rfc3161} for requesting and receiving timestamp tokens from a TSA.

| TST
| **Time-Stamp Token.** The CMS `SignedData` structure returned by a TSA containing a `TSTInfo` with the timestamp time and the hash of the timestamped data.

| VRI
| **Validation Related Information.** A sub-dictionary of the DSS that groups certificates, CRLs, and OCSP responses for a specific signature. Keyed by the SHA-1 hash of the signature value.

| X.509
| The ITU-T standard for public-key certificates. Version 3 (X.509 v3) is used universally in PDF signatures. Defined in {rfc5280}.

| XAdES
| **XML Advanced Electronic Signatures.** ETSI standard (EN 319 132) for XML-based signatures. Not directly relevant to PDF signatures, but part of the same eIDAS framework as PAdES and CAdES.

[TIP]
====
The RFCs are freely available and are the definitive reference for CMS, X.509, OCSP, and TSP.
The ETSI standards are available free of charge from ETSI's website after registration.
ISO 32000-2 is a paid standard, but much of the PDF signature specification is reproduced (with commentary) in this book and in Adobe's freely published PDF Reference documents.
